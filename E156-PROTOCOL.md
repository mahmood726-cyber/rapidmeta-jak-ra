# E156 Protocol — JAK inhibitors for ACR20 in rheumatoid arthritis

- **Project:** rapidmeta-jak-ra (Living Meta-Analysis #105)
- **Built:** 2026-06-08
- **Dashboard:** https://mahmood726-cyber.github.io/rapidmeta-jak-ra/
- **Primary estimand:** Pooled odds ratio for ACR20 response, oral JAK inhibitor vs placebo.

## Body (7-sentence E156)
S1 — In adults with active rheumatoid arthritis, do oral JAK inhibitors increase the odds of an ACR20 response compared with placebo across pivotal phase-3 trials? S2 — We synthesised four placebo-controlled phase-3 RCTs (ORAL Solo, RA-BEAM, SELECT-NEXT, FINCH 1; 2,728 analysed participants) of tofacitinib, baricitinib, upadacitinib, and filgotinib. S3 — ACR20 responder counts from each trial's ClinicalTrials.gov results record were pooled as odds ratios by inverse-variance random-effects (REML), with I², Cochran's Q, an HKSJ-adjusted interval, and a t-based prediction interval. S4 — The pooled odds ratio was 3.40 (95% CI 2.89 to 3.99), i.e. ACR20 response was roughly 3.4 times more likely with a JAK inhibitor than placebo. S5 — Between-trial heterogeneity was absent (I² = 0%, Q(3) = 0.74, p = 0.86; τ² = 0), and the 95% prediction interval (2.61 to 4.42) excluded no benefit. S6 — Despite differing background therapy and ACR20 timepoints, all four agents showed a concordant, large, statistically significant ACR20 advantage. S7 — As a class-level living synthesis of one pivotal trial per agent, it does not capture dose-ranging, head-to-head, or longer-term/safety outcomes, and is updated as further trials are added.
